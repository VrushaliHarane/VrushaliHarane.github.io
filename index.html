About me

A little about who I am

I'm a software engineer who loves the intersection of distributed systems and machine learning infrastructure. Right now I'm finishing my MS at UC Davis, where I've been researching RDMA-based training primitives on GPU clusters — but my background spans backend engineering, cloud-scale systems, and generative AI.

Before grad school, I spent three years at Walmart Labs, where I grew from building resilient APIs to leading a team of three engineers on a high-impact microservice for return logistics — work that earned me a company-wide Excellence Award among 75 engineers.

I interned at AWS the summer of 2025, where I built a RAG-based AI agent that could summarize contracts in under 30 seconds across a million-plus documents. That project reinforced my belief that good systems design — not just clever models — is what makes AI practical at scale.

Outside of work, I enjoy mentoring students (I was a TA for 90+ people at UC Davis), attending Grace Hopper Celebration, and finding ways to make technical concepts more accessible.
